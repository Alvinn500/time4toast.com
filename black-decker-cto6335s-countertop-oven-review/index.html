Black & Decker CTO6335S Countertop Oven Review – One Touch Cooking

April 16, 2015 by Audrey 2 Comments

Black & Decker kitchen appliances are generally affordable and relatively cheaper than its major competitors for a product with the same features. The Black & Decker CTO6335S countertop convection oven is no different. It is popular for its reasonable pricing although there are also weaknesses in the product with room for improvement. Check out the review below to see if it is the oven that will suit your needs.

Black & Decker CTO6335S Review

The features of this oven are:

  • 1500-watt
  • Digital control and display
  • One-touch cooking
  • Convection cooking
  • Timer
  • Start/Stop button
  • Automatic shut-off
  • Nonstick interior
  • Stainless steel exterior
  • Glass door
  • Removable crumb tray
  • Size: 22.8” x 13.4” x 15.5”

==> Check Price

The preset cooking functions of this 1500-watt oven are many – cookies, pizza, frozen snacks, potato, bake and broil. When these buttons are pressed, the preset time and temperature will operate for quick one-touch cooking.

You can change the temperature by pressing the up or down arrow as required once a cooking function has been selected. The cooking time can also be re-adjusted with the timer button. Pressing the toast and bagel buttons will allow you to change the shade setting from 1 to 7. This has also been preset to 4 (medium) for your convenience.

The Black & Decker CTO6335S has a convection feature and when activated, you will be able to hear the fan running with the appropriate symbol appearing on the display panel. This feature is used with the bake function.

You can select from 2 height levels for the wire rack. The interior can fit a 12” pizza or about 6 slices of bread. When a cycle is complete, the oven will beep 3 times. The cooking will automatically stop or you can halt it mid-cycle by pressing the Start/Stop button.

The exterior is brushed stainless steel while the interior has a nonstick surface. The wire rack and baking pan can be washed in the dishwasher. The crumb tray is removable from the bottom and can be cleaned with warm, sudsy water.

Should You Buy This Black & Decker Countertop Convection Oven?

Pros
  1. Automatic settings – The one-touch cooking for common items like potato and pizza makes this oven quick and easy to use. Another model which has this convenience is the Oster TSSTTVMNDG.
  2. Price is reasonable – For the number of functions and the convection feature, the price of the Black & Decker CTO6335S is certainly within the affordable range. Amazon is selling it for about $80 at the time of this review. As comparison, the Breville BOV450XL Mini Smart Oven sells for nearly $150 and it is smaller in size.
  3. Large interior – The interior can fit a whole chicken which is nice if you are cooking for a small family.
  4. Even heating – The oven heats up quickly and food comes out evenly cooked. Most buyers are satisfied with the cooking performance of the unit.
Cons
  1. Gets too hot – Some customers have complained that the oven gets too hot on the outside when it is in operation making it risky to use. Actually, all countertop ovens do get hot but some could be hotter than others. So, leave enough space around the top and sides of the oven on your countertop if you are planning to buy this and be careful during use.
  2. No door hinge – The oven door drops down then you open it as there is no hinge for support.
  3. Display panel not lighted – A few users do not like the LCD display which has no backlight. Hence, settings can be difficult to read when it is dark. For a toaster oven with a illuminated digital display, you can take a look at the Cuisinart TOB-100.

This model is more than a toaster oven unlike the Black and Decker TO1303SB which is far cheaper. But if you are going to cook rather than just toast, this is a more suitable option.

The price is attractive for the Black & Decker CTO6335S which makes it a worthwhile buy, if you can tolerate the heat coming from the oven given that it is a common complaint among users. Some people find it to be a big issue while others don’t, so we see it more as a personal preference.

Also, bear in mind that this Black & Decker countertop oven uses a digital display that requires you to be quick in keying in your settings. Once a cooking function is selected, it gives you 10 seconds to change the temperature and time. Any slower than that, it will revert to the default setting and you will have to start all over again. Hence, this may not be the ideal oven for the elderly who might find this a little complicated compared to using plain old knobs for the settings like those found on the Cuisinart TOB-40.
