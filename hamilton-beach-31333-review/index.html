Hamilton Beach 31333 Review – Toaster Oven With A Large Glass Door

March 13, 2015 //  by Audrey//  Leave a Comment

The Hamilton Beach 31333 is a mid-size toaster oven that is fairly affordable. It is marketed as a unit with easy access to food. With so many brands available, it is to be expected that there has to be a unique selling feature in each model to attract buyers. In this Hamilton Beach 31333 convection toaster oven review, we will highlight the nice features of this model and the flaws that might make you think twice about its suitability.

What Is The Main Selling Point Of The Hamilton Beach 31333?

The main selling point is it is designed for easy access to food. What this means is that the rack has an auto-slide out mechanism to make it easier to reach your food. While many other models have this same feature (the Cuisinart TOB-40 is an example), it also has a large curved glass door for greater maneuverability when accessing your food. When the door is pulled down, you are less likely to scrape your hands against the door when you reach your hands into the oven with its curved design.

Other features of the Hamilton Beach 31333 include:

  • 6-slice capacity – The interior measurements are 12.2″ (W) x 12.5″ (D) x 7″ (H). If measured from the rack to the top, you have about 5″ height to work with. With this size, you can fit a 12″ pizza in the oven as the back is also curved to give you that extra room. The outer dimensions are 18″ (W) x 14″ (D) x 9″ (H).
  • Convection – The unit has a convection feature to reduce cooking time by about one-third compared to traditional baking. Apart from convection, the other cooking modes are toast, bake and broil. Another 6-slice oven with convection cooking is the Waring Pro TCO650.
  • 2 rack positions – There are 2 slots for the rack to cater for different food to give you more meal options.
  • Stay on feature – The timer is only for 30 minutes. For longer cooking times, there is the convenient stay on feature to avoid the hassle of having to re-set the timer. The Black & Decker TO3210SSD also has this same practical feature.
  • Front access crumb tray – The removable crumb tray can be accessed from the front below the door. You don’t have to access it through the interior of the oven which can result in the crumbs spilling onto the door at times.

Hamilton Beach 31333 Vs 31331

As far as we can see, both models have the same features and measurements. The only noticeable difference is the color whereby the 31333 model has a silver outer casing and trimmings compared to the 31331 which has a black exterior.

The two units also enjoy similar ratings from buyers on Amazon although the silver model seems to be the more popular choice with a better sales rank in its category.

Should You Buy The Hamilton Beach 31333 Convection Toaster Oven?

Pros
  1. Economical – For a 6-slice toaster oven with convection cooking, it is economically priced. At the time of this review, it is retailing at around $60 on Amazon. The Oster TSSTTVRB04 is selling for $80 while the DeLonghi EO1270 is even more expensive at over $90. Its price level is comparable to Black & Decker models though and they are all ideal for the budget conscious.
  2. Accurate temperature – Quite few buyers have noted that the actual temperature in the oven is very close to the settings. The temperature range from 150°F to 450°F. It heats up quickly and can cook a variety of food with great results.
  3. Full glass door – Not many countertop ovens have a full glass door like this model does. Most have trimmings at the sides. One of the benefits of this large curved glass door is that it enables you to easily check on your food while it is cooking.
Cons
  1. Difficult to set toast shade – There is a fine line between untoasted and charred and some users have complained that it is hard to get the shade right. The range between light and dark is also too small in the settings to get that perfect toasts.
  2. Convection function fails – This is another complaint raised by some consumers. The convection function breaks down after a year or so which is a disappointment.
  3. Rack doesn’t slide out – While the product is marketed as having an auto-slide out rack, some buyers seem to have ended up with a unit that has no such feature. The door also doesn’t close tightly for a few buyers as the auto-slide out rack is not perfectly constructed and gets in the way when you are trying to shut it.

This Hamilton Beach unit is one of the cheapest convection toaster ovens in the market but buying it seems to be a little risky. From the many customer reviews we have read, buyers tend to end up loving or hating it. For some, it is perfect given the affordable price. For others, it doesn’t last and they can never get their toast shade right.

The Hamilton Beach 31333 is certainly not the best toaster oven but neither is it the worst. However, if you are buying it for the convection function, then we suggest that you look at other alternatives (e.g. Black & Decker CTO6335S) since the complaints of it failing has been noted. Weighing about 12 lbs, it is not exactly a solid, well-made oven. So, don’t expect a lot from this unit since it is more of a budget model.
