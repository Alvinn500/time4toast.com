Panasonic NB-G110P Flash Xpress Review – Best Infrared Toaster Oven

March 5, 2015 //  by Audrey//  Leave a Comment

In an effort to reduce cooking time, manufacturers have been tinkering with the heating elements to satisfy customer demand for speed. For the Panasonic NB-G110P Flash Xpress toaster oven, a fast cooking time is certainly one of its main selling points. Combined with its small footprint, it is a very efficient appliance that should be a great help in your kitchen. The review below will give you a better idea of the workings of this oven and why it might be the best infrared toaster oven in the market.

How Much Faster Is The Panasonic Flash Xpress?

The manufacturer boasts of a 40% faster cooking time with this model compared to conventional toaster ovens. This is achieved through the double infrared light of two quartz and ceramic heating elements placed both on the front and back of the oven. With no pre-heating required, you can cut down on your cooking time substantially while still enjoying evenly cooked food. The different location heating elements work in tandem to brown food on the outside and penetrate it to efficiently cook the inside as well resulting in even results.

Other features of this unit that are of interest to a prospective buyer include:

  • 6 auto-cook menus – What this means is that you already have 6 pre-programmed cooking modes and 5 shades of browning built in. Simply press the easily understandable touch-button icons to operate and your food will be ready when the oven beeps. Simple to use for the average user as there would be no more guesswork needed for the cooking time and heat level.
  • Precise temperature control – The temperature range is fully displayed in both Fahrenheit and Celcius. It goes from 120°C (250°F) to 260°C (500°F) in 20°C increments. The benefit of this design is that temperature adjustment will be quick and precise using a left and right arrow button.
  • Interior light – A lot of times, we just want to check on the progress of our food. But not many models come with an interior light that allows us to do just that. For the Panasonic Flash Xpress toaster oven, you can turn on the light to see inside clearly so that your food would not end up being overdone. Press the bulb icon button again, and the light will turn off. Another model that also has an interior light is the Cuisinart TOB-260 Chef’s Convection toaster oven.
  • Auto-eject rack – This is another much sought after feature in a toaster oven. The see-through door is connected to the rack and hence, when the door is opened, the rack will automatically slide out. This means easy loading and unloading of food with the rack pulled out and the door laid flat.

What Is The Size Of The Panasonic NB-G110P?

The dimensions for the Panasonic NB-G110P are 13″ (W) x 12″ (D) x 10.25″ (H). It weighs about 7.5 lbs. The interior can fit a 9″ pizza or 4 slices of bread. A 9″ x 9″ square pan is all that it can take in. The interior measurements are 10.2″ (W) x 9.8″ D) x 4.1″ (H).

The power usage is 1300 watts. Included in the package is a square inner tray and front access removable crumb tray and the available colors are white (NB-G110PW) and silver.

Should You Buy This Panasonic Toaster Oven?

Pros
  1. Toasts evenly – The compliments from users are numerous when it comes to the toasting results. This oven does a nice job of browning the outside to reflect an even shade on both sides of the bread. Some toaster ovens will give you a darker shade on one side compared to the other but not this Panasonic unit. The toasts are also crispy with consistent results each time. With good reviews overall and high ratings from buyers, it certainly qualifies as one of the best toaster ovens in the market.
  2. Cheaper than the Breville – The other model with high ratings and similar size is the Breville BOV450XL Mini Smart Oven but this one costs $40 to $50 more at the time of this Panasonic NB-G110P Flash Xpress toaster oven review. However, the Breville unit has a broil function with higher power consumption of 1800 watts. So, if you don’t have a need for the broil function, you can save some money with the Panasonic oven instead.
  3. Compact in size – This is a perfect fit for those who don’t have the luxury of countertop space. One of the smallest toaster ovens around with a good rating to boot, it is about 1″ to 2″ narrower than most other compact models in the market. For example, the Cuisinart TOB-80 Compact Toaster Oven Broiler is about 15″ wide compared to 13″ for the Panasonic Flash Xpress.
  4. Faster cooking time – The toaster oven does not require pre-heating and this alone would save you considerable time. Most buyers have commented that the oven works fast and you can toast a bagel in under a minute. It definitely works faster than a conventional model for a lot of buyers out there.
Cons
  1. No broiling – This unit does not have the broil feature which is a standard for toaster ovens of this price range. The manufacturer also recommends that meat be wrapped up in aluminum foil to prevent oil from dripping onto the heating elements and causing a flare-up. Neither does this oven has a convection feature which people might come to expect from a $100 unit. Even the cheaper Black & Decker CTO6335S Stainless Steel Countertop Oven has both broil and convection functions for more cooking options.
  2. Short timer – The digital timer can be set up to a maximum of 25 minutes and thus, you will have to re-set the timer for recipes with a longer cooking time. This is far shorter than the usual 60 minutes and would be an inconvenience for some.
  3. Bright light – The heating elements emit a bright light which goes on and off as it regulates the temperature inside the oven. Some people have found this annoying and a pain to the eyes.

The conclusion that we can draw from this is that the oven is suitable for those who are using it mainly for toasting, reheating and cooking of simple meals. After all, it is too small for larger cuts of meat and it is not designed for cooking these types of recipes. So, if you are looking for an appliance that will toast your bread, heat up frozen pizzas and chicken nuggets and re-heat leftovers straight out from the fridge, this is the right choice. It is the best infrared toaster oven for this type of simple meals.

But if you are going to cook much more than that, then there are certainly better, cheaper and more suitable choices than this Panasonic NB-G110P Flash Xpress toaster oven. We recommend it for its small footprint, quickness and ease of use for simple meals. But for larger meals and more complex recipes, you will have to look elsewhere.
