Black & Decker TO1332SBD Review – 4-Slice Toaster Oven

March 11, 2015 //  by Audrey//  Leave a Comment

The Black & Decker TO1332SBD 4-slice toaster oven is hugely popular with its attractive selling price. It is not the best toaster oven out there but many people do find it sufficient for their daily needs. Would it work out for you? In this review below, we will examine the features in more detail and check out what most buyers have to say about this appliance to help you make up your mind.

Does The Black & Decker TO1332SBD Toast Evenly?

According to the manufacturer, this model has the exclusive Even Toast technology in its design. So, it should be able to toast up to 30% more evenly compared to other leading brands in the market. It does this by optimizing the interior heat distribution. The majority of buyers are satisfied with how their toasts turned out using this oven. The shade is not perfectly even on both sides but the toasts do come out crispy.

==> Check Price

Apart from the Even Toast technology, other features of the oven that are worth mentioning are:

  • 4 modes of operation – Bake, broil, toast and keep warm are the 4 cooking modes available. You choose the desired function by turning the top dial which will also control the temperature. With only 2 control dials, the oven is intuitive to use. There is certainly no necessity to pore over the manual for complicated settings often found on digital models. Another unit which only has 2 dials as controls is the Proctor Silex 4-slice toaster oven.
  • Timer – A 30-minute timer is built in for convenience. For a longer baking time, you can also opt for the stay on function. In other words, you don’t have to come back to re-set the timer again if you plan to leave your food cooking in the oven for longer than 30 minutes. The Black & Decker TO1303SB also has this same stay on function, a model that is very similar to this TO1332SBD.
  • Curved interior – The curved interior gives you more space to so that you can fit a 9″ pizza into the oven. You can also toast up to 4 slices of bread at one go although it is a snug fit. The size of the Black & Decker TO1332SBD is 15.5″ (W) x 11″ (D) x 9″ (H). The interior measurements are about 10″ (W) x 8″ (D). This is where the curved interior gives you a little bit more space for your pizza. The height from the rack to the top heating element is about 3″.

Should You Buy The Black & Decker TO1332SBD 4-Slice Toaster Oven?

Pros
  1. Acceptable toast time – The oven does not take too long to heat up. Your toast can be ready in about 4 to 5 minutes which is acceptably fast given that it only has one heating element each at the top and bottom. It consumes 1150 watts of power which is much lower than the high end models like the Breville BOV450XL Mini Smart Oven which uses 1800 watts. For the Breville, it would take about 3 minutes to get your toast done but you are also paying much more for it.
  2. Affordable pricing – For less than $40, you can bring home this Black & Decker toaster oven at the time of this review. It is not the cheapest but more of a mid-range model for a 4-slice capacity. The lowest end units will generally sell for less than $30. So, you are paying about $10 more but you will be buying an appliance with a reputable brand name in exchange.
  3. Cooks fine – The oven heats up properly and food like nuggets, fish fillets, potatoes and pizzas come out nicely cooked. Simple to use, it is fine for preparing basic stuff.
Cons
  1. Burns toast – There is a trial and error involved when you first use the Black & Decker TO1332SBD. There is a tendency to get your toast charred as some buyers have experienced. This is because there is a very narrow window between looking untoasted and having a shade of golden brown. If you missed that window with your setting, your bread will get burnt pretty quickly. What this means is that you will have to carefully monitor the toasting to prevent burning rather than having the luxury of leaving it unattended and relying on the timer.
  2. Rack stays inside – The rack does not slide out with the door when opened. A few users have had food slid down from the rack when it was accidentally pulled too far out. So, you do need to be careful when sliding out the rack manually to retrieve your food. The Cuisinart TOB-80 is an example of a 4-slice toaster oven with an auto-eject rack.
  3. Can’t adjust rack height – You are stuck with only one level for the rack which limits the type of food you can cook in it. The Black & Decker TRO480BS would have 2 rack heights if you prefer an oven with more flexibility.

Our verdict is, if you have high expectations for your toaster oven, the Black & Decker TO1332SBD won’t leave you satisfied. The toast shade setting could be more precise to better prevent burnt toasts and it lacks a convection feature for faster baking. You might also prefer a front access removable crumb tray and more than one rack height position for added convenience.

The bottomline is there are no nice surprises where the Black & Decker TO1332SBD is concerned. It works as it should for an appliance of this price range but there are no additional features to make you feel excited about. If you are happy with just a few basic functions and do not mind a mid-quality appliance, then go for it. Basically, it is not a great toaster oven but just good for the price.
