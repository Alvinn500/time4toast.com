Breville BOV800XL Review – The Smart Oven

May 18, 2015 //  by Audrey//  Leave a Comment

Some people seem to think that a countertop oven is only good for making toast and re-heating pizzas. For more complex meals, they think that they still need to power up the stove oven. Well, this is where they are wrong. The Breville BOV800XL Smart Oven can replace your big oven with its many functions and this review will list out its features below. The only limitation with a countertop oven is its size but for everyday meals, it is more than sufficient for most families’ needs.

Breville BOV800XL Smart Oven Review

The features of this Breville Smart Oven are as follows:

  • 1800-watt
  • 9 menu functions
  • Defrost feature
  • Temperature conversion
  • Nonstick interior coating
  • Timer
  • LCD display
  • Convection cooking
  • Removable crumb tray
  • Auto-rack eject
  • Element IQ feature
  • 5 quartz heating elements
  • Size: 18.5” (W) x 16.25” (D) x 11.25” (H)

==> Check Price

The Breville BOV800XL is an 1800-watt smart oven with 9 menu functions. These functions are basically pre-set programs for different types of cooking methods – toast, bagel, bake, roast, broil, pizza, cookies, reheat and warm. The oven is smart in the sense that it is able to adjust the heat accordingly and distribute it to the right place at the right time through the Element IQ technology.

The 5 quartz heating elements provide for efficient cooking. The BOV800XL heats quickly and evenly and wattage will increase or decrease according to the functions set to give off different levels of heat for different cooking methods.

The size of the Breville Smart Oven is neither too big nor too small. Inside, it can fit 6 slices of bread or a 13” pizza with 3 rack positions. There are indications on the oven door to let you know the rack height for different types of cooking. You then use a dial to choose your cooking method which you can see on the LCD display when you turn it on. Below are two other dials – a temperature control and timer. Both allow you to change the programmed settings and the oven will save the changes in its memory for next use. There is also a temperature conversion button (°F/°C) for easy reference.

You can view the Breville toaster oven out of the box here:

Breville BOV800XL vs BOV650XL vs BOV450XL

The BOV800XL is, of course, has the most complete functions. Apart from keeping warm and convection cooking, it also has an additional heating element. Larger in size, it can fit 2 more slices of bread compared to the other two smaller models. A baking pan, pizza pan and broil rack are included with the BOV800XL.

The Breville BOV650XL fits a 12” pizza or 4 slices of bread. This is the in-between model – good for a small family and you are also offered a baking pan and broil rack as accessories.

The smallest model, the BOV450XL, can only accommodate an 11” pizza. But it has the “a bit more” feature which is not found on the 2 larger models. This button allows you to cook something a tad longer once a cycle has been completed if you find your food still needs a little more cooking time before it is done.

Should You Buy The Breville BOV800XL Smart Oven?

Pros
  1. Auto-rack eject – This feature is a little different from other toaster ovens because it uses a magnet to draw the rack out half-way when you open the oven door. The magnet on the side of the door draws the rack out together with it when you pull it open. Not all toaster ovens have a rack that slides out automatically, so having one is a convenient feature. Another brand with an auto slide out rack is the Cuisinart TOB-100 Compact Digital Toaster Oven Broiler.
  2. All knobs – There are no up and down arrow buttons to fiddle with when you need to change the cooking methods, time and temperature. Knobs are quick and easy to use and you can get to the right settings easier and quicker.
  3. Large LCD display – The display panel is easy to read with a backlight. It lights up with a blue background when it is turned on and changes to orange when it is cooking so that you can easily know at a glance if your food is ready or otherwise.
  4. Alternative to the big oven – Quite a few buyers have mentioned that the Breville BOV800XL has replaced their big oven and helped them save on energy. Basically, you can do your daily cooking using this countertop oven and save the big oven for really large items for special occasions like a turkey. Majority of buyers said that their meals turned out great and tasty with this appliance and the toast is evenly shaded and crispy. For an even larger unit, you can take a look at the Oster TSSTTVDGXL-SHP which can fit over 10 slices of bread per batch.
Cons
  1. Highly priced – The price for this Element IQ oven is about $250 at the time of review. This is certainly steep when you can choose from so many other models for $100 less. Even the Cuisinart TOB-200 is cheaper with 12 cooking functions including rotisserie.
  2. No interior light – For the price, having an interior light would be a nice addition for the oven. For this feature, you will have to get the Cuisinart TOB-260 or the Oster TSSTTVMNDG.
  3. Fan is noisy – A few users have noted that the fan is a little noisy when the convection function is turned on.

From customer reviews on major online retailers’ websites, we have read how buyers have never thought they would spend over $200 on a toaster oven. But they ended up believing that it is worth every penny. This is because the oven does not disappoint in its performance and construction quality. Users are happy with their cooking results and because the unit can take over most of the functions of the big oven, they feel it is a good buy and the best toaster oven out there.

Overall, you get what you pay for with the Breville BOV800XL Smart Oven. It is a high investment but worth it, if you are going to use it daily for your 3 meals. Complaints of defects and malfunctions are also few and far in between to give you even more comfort in your buying decision.
